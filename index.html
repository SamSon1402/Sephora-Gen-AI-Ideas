Team Energy Heatmap

Team Energy Heatmap

Overview

The Team Energy Heatmap is an AI-powered visualization tool that analyzes and displays the optimal meeting times for global teams across multiple time zones. By leveraging the Microsoft Graph API, we collect anonymized activity data to determine when team members are most alert, productive, and available.

How It Works

Our algorithm analyzes several data points from Microsoft 365 to create an accurate energy profile for each team member:

  • Calendar activity patterns
  • Email and Teams message time distributions
  • Document editing peak times
  • Self-reported productivity data
  • Meeting participation metrics

The system identifies optimal "golden hours" when team members across different regions are most likely to be at peak energy levels.

Microsoft Integration

This feature integrates directly with Microsoft Teams and Outlook to provide actionable meeting time suggestions when scheduling. The system automatically suggests optimal meeting times that respect everyone's energy patterns, not just their availability.

Burnout Prevention Shield

Overview

The Burnout Prevention Shield is our AI-powered early warning system that helps identify and prevent employee burnout before it happens. Using advanced pattern recognition and sentiment analysis, this tool achieves an impressive 94% accuracy in detecting burnout risk factors.

How It Works

Our AI model analyzes several key indicators from Microsoft 365 data to identify potential burnout patterns:

  • After-hours work frequency and duration
  • Meeting overload patterns
  • Email response time changes
  • Collaboration pattern disruptions
  • Communication sentiment analysis
  • Calendar fragmentation

When the system detects potential burnout risks, it provides personalized recommendations to both the employee and their manager (with privacy controls).

Personalized Recommendations

Example: Meeting Overload Alert
"You've had 28% more meetings this month compared to your optimal baseline. Consider blocking focus time on Tuesdays and Thursdays, which historically have been your most productive days."
Example: After-Hours Work Pattern
"Your team has been consistently working after 7pm for the past two weeks. Consider rescheduling morning meetings to allow for later start times."
